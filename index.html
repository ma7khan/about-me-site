ABOUT ME

My name is Monia Bhutta-Khan. I am 19 years old, and currently in my 2nd year of the Global Business and Digital Arts Program at the University of Waterloo.

I love to spend time with family and friends, travel, play the piano, and learn more about the world of digital arts.

Over the years I have had the opportunity to delve into various mediums of art that have allowed me to develop my skills and expand my portfolio. I enjoy different creative outlets that help me express how I feel, and allow me to be my authentic self. Follow my journey as a videographer, photographer and spoken word artist.

SPOKEN WORD

I am drawn to the art of spoken word because it has become my safe space to create and express myself freely with no judgements. It allows me to express my innermost thoughts in a poetic way, using imagery and metaphors without having to outright say how I feel if I donâ€™t want to. At first I kept my spoken words in the notes app on my phone, me being the only person to read them. But, I started performing my spoken words in 2018 at different youth open mic events. At first I was nervous to speak and share my innermost thoughts and feelings with the world, but soon I loved performing and couldn't get enough of it. It felt very powerful to stand up on stage in front of a bunch of strangers and speak my truth. I also started posting my pieces on Instagram and TikTok, mostly so that others know they are not alone in how they feel about certain situations. It has become this great community where I and other artists encourage one another to share our work and speak our truths. I've chosen to share a selection of my spoken words and poems in the same format I initially shared them in on my Instagram. I've chosen to present them through screenshots, capturing the essence of their origin and staying true to their authentic form.

PHOTOGRAPHY

I started photography at the age of 12 because I always found myself taking photos of nature on my parents phone, and eventually mine when I got one in grade 6. In grade 7 my parents bought me a camera, a sony a6000, which is still the camera I use to this day. My dad chose this camera because it was known to be good for videography, and he knew I wanted to get into that eventually as well. He also knew it was on the lighter side, meaning if I took it on our vacations with us, it wouldn't be too heavy to carry around. I started out doing a lot of nature photography and architecture photography when we went on big Europe trips in the summer, and them other tropical vacations since then.

When covid hit and we couldn't travel anymore, I was forced to step outside of my comfort zone and delve into different types of photography. I started to transition into portrait and fashion photography in 2019. At first I did shoots for fun, to see what my creative expression was capable of, making my sister and friends be my subject. Eventually as I gained more followers on Instagram I started doing shoots for people who would DM me and ask for my business. I focused on doing shoots that uplifted other artists such as my rapper, singer, poet and model friends. I wanted to use my platform to uplift them and provide them the space to be seen by a larger community. I also had the opportunity to shoot for a youth fashion magazine, having my work featured in a magazine for the first time!

Fashion and portrait photography became my outlet to express my creativity by planning shoots; choosing outfits, backdrops or photoshoot locations, planning poses, making a mood board and a playlist. All of these things allow me to make my vision come to life. Below you'll see a variety of fashion and portrait photos I have taken over the years since 2019.

These photos are photos I took of family and friends, wanting to try out different locations, outfits and concepts.

These are photos I took for a youth-run fashion magazine called Contrapposto.

At the same time, in 2020, I started taking photos at protests such as BLM protests, defund the police protests and free Palestine protests. I still enjoy nature photography from time to time, but I now focus more on fashion and activism photography.

Activitsm photography allows me to capture the essence of a movement, the power in peoples faces and voices and they chant, protest and march. I am able to capture photos of thier signs and spread thier messages beyond that moment in time, sharing it on social media, spreading the word.

VIDEOGRAPHY

I started getting into videography in 2020, attending protests for Palestine and BLM. Not only did I photograph, but I also recorded the march, speeches, chants and posters, with the intention to make a highlights video to use my platform to uplift the messages of the Black Community. I really enjoy the process of editing, matching clips to music, and visual story telling. This led me into developping the skills to become a known videorapher in the last year of highschool (2021/2022 school year). I was asked by different schools conferences and clubs to attend thier events and create highlights videos of the events. Below you'll see a couple videos I have uploaded to YouTube, the ones I am most proud of. From there I have gone into shooting and making reels/tiktoks as highlights, and hope to continue to use my skills and platform to be an ally to marginalized communities.
